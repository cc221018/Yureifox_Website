The Golden Fridge

The Golden Fridge, also known as TGF, is an Android app with a virtual fridge which enables users to organize their real fridge better, get an overview of what’s currently inside, to plan meals in advance. Ultimately, the app aims to help users reduce their food waste and raise the awareness about the global food waste problem. The finished product is a result of the final 2 project weeks of my third semester, also known as the “Creative CodeLabs 3” - done in teams of 2 people.

Setting up the Project

The Creative CodeLabs (CCL) occur at the semester's end, requiring me to apply all the knowledge acquired throughout my courses to craft a final product. For this project, it meant donning the hats of a designer, user experience evaluater, (mobile) front-end and back-end developer, and user tester. Since there was only limited time to code, my project partner and me had to come up with a rather simple design for our app.

Design Variants

Simplicity wasn’t the only thing I wanted to incorporate into our app. I aimed to infuse it with an intuitive, fresh ambiance, given its focus on food. A touch of playfulness was also incorporated to inspire users to engage with the app more frequently. Our exploration with various colors led us to create multiple versions, allowing us to decide on which one fitted the most.

The Fridge

For our app, my partner and I wanted to create something simple, but intuitive and playful. We asked ourselves, how could we give a user the feel of actually organizing their own fridge? To achieve this, we introduced a virtual fridge as the main screen, featuring various categories of food. Users can easily add new items to any category, and for efficiency, we implemented color indicators to swiftly convey information, showing whether a category is empty, or an item is overdue.

Informative Overview

The second main screen serves as a comprehensive information hub. Most importantly and thus pinned at the top, it highlights overdue items in the user's fridge to promote mindful consumption and reduce food waste. As users scroll down the Overview page, they encounter articles addressing diverse food waste topics, aiming to raise awareness and offer valuable knowledge and tips. The final section showcases a history of all items used up by the user, providing a comprehensive overview of their consumption patterns.

User Testing

Upon completing our designs, my project partner and I conducted usability testing, evaluating our app against 10 usability heuristics. This process enabled us to identify pain points and areas in need of further testing. Formulating experimental questions and hypotheses, we crafted a user test with 5 tasks and subsequently conducted it, involving 8 users. Through careful documentation and analysis of the results, we were able to vastly improve our app.
